Senate Bill No. 243

Senate Bill No. 243

Passed the Senate September 11, 2025

CHAPTER

An act to add Chapter 22.6 (commencing with Section 22601) to Division 8 of the Business and Professions Code, relating to artificial intelligence.

LEGISLATIVE COUNSEL'S DIGEST

SB 243, Padilla. Companion chatbots.

Existing law requires a social media platform to take various steps to prevent cyberbullying of minors on the platform, including by requiring the platform to establish a prominent mechanism within its internet-based service that allows any individual, whether or not that individual has a profile on the internet-based service, to report cyberbullying or any content that violates the existing terms of service related to cyberbullying. Existing law authorizes the State Department of Public Health to establish the Office of Suicide Prevention in the department, as prescribed.

This bill would, among other things related to making a companion chatbot platform safer for users, if a reasonable person interacting with a companion chatbot would be misled to believe that the person is interacting with a human, require an operator of a companion chatbot platform to issue a clear and conspicuous notification indicating that the companion chatbot is artificially generated and not human. The bill would also require an operator to take certain actions with respect to a user the operator knows is a minor, including disclose to the user that the user is interacting with artificial intelligence. The bill would also require an operator to prevent a companion chatbot on its companion chatbot platform from engaging with users unless the operator maintains a protocol for preventing the production of suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm content to the user, as specified, and would require an operator to publish details on that protocol on the operator's internet website.

This bill would, beginning July 1, 2027, require an operator to annually report to the Office of Suicide Prevention certain things, including protocols put in place to detect, remove, and respond to instances of suicidal ideation by users, and would require the office to post data from that report on its internet website. The bill would authorize a person who suffers injury in fact as a result of noncompliance with the bill to bring a certain civil action.

The people of the State of California do enact as follows:

SECTION 1. Chapter 22.6 (commencing with Section 22601) is added to Division 8 of the Business and Professions Code, to read:
Chapter 22.6. Companion Chatbots
22601. Definitions

As used in this chapter:

  • "Artificial intelligence" means an engineered or machine-based system that varies in its level of autonomy and that can, for explicit or implicit objectives, infer from the input it receives how to generate outputs that can influence physical or virtual environments.SCOPE: Defines what qualifies as "artificial intelligence" under this law. This definition determines which systems fall within the regulatory framework.
  • (1) "Companion chatbot" means an artificial intelligence system with a natural language interface that provides adaptive, human-like responses to user inputs and is capable of meeting a user's social needs, including by exhibiting anthropomorphic features and being able to sustain a relationship across multiple interactions.SCOPE: Defines what qualifies as a "companion chatbot" - the primary subject of this law. Key triggers: natural language interface, adaptive/human-like responses, meets social needs, anthropomorphic features, sustains relationships across interactions.
  • (2) "Companion chatbot" does not include any of the following:EXCEPTION: Safe harbor provision - these types of bots are explicitly excluded from the law's requirements, even if they might otherwise meet the companion chatbot definition.
    • (A) A bot that is used only for customer service, a business' operational purposes, productivity and analysis related to source information, internal research, or technical assistance.EXCEPTION: Business-focused bots used for operational purposes are exempt. This protects enterprise software and customer service tools.
    • (B) A bot that is a feature of a video game and is limited to replies related to the video game that cannot discuss topics related to mental health, self-harm, sexually explicit conduct, or maintain a dialogue on other topics unrelated to the video game.EXCEPTION: Video game NPCs/characters are exempt IF they stay within game-related topics and don't discuss mental health, self-harm, or sexual content. Once they go beyond game topics, this exemption may not apply.
    • (C) A stand-alone consumer electronic device that functions as a speaker and voice command interface, acts as a voice-activated virtual assistant, and does not sustain a relationship across multiple interactions or generate outputs that are likely to elicit emotional responses in the user.EXCEPTION: Smart speakers/voice assistants (like Alexa, Google Home) are exempt if they don't sustain relationships or elicit emotional responses. Task-oriented voice assistants are protected.
  • "Companion chatbot platform" means a platform that allows a user to engage with companion chatbots.SCOPE: Defines the platform that hosts companion chatbots. The operator of this platform is the regulated entity.
  • "Office" means the Office of Suicide Prevention established pursuant to Section 131300 of the Health and Safety Code.
  • "Operator" means a person who makes a companion chatbot platform available to a user in the state.SCOPE: Defines who is regulated. Applies to anyone who makes a companion chatbot platform available to California users. Geographic trigger: users "in the state" of California.
  • "Sexually explicit conduct" has the meaning defined in Section 2256 of Title 18 of the United States Code.
  • "Video game" means a game played on an electronic amusement device that utilizes a computer, microprocessor, or similar electronic circuitry and its own monitor, or is designed to be used with a television set or a computer monitor, that interacts with the user of the device.
22602. Operator Requirements

(a) If a reasonable person interacting with a companion chatbot would be misled to believe that the person is interacting with a human, an operator shall issue a clear and conspicuous notification indicating that the companion chatbot is artificially generated and not human.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST provide clear disclosure when the chatbot could mislead users into thinking it's human. Trigger: "reasonable person" test - if a typical user would be confused, disclosure is required.

(b) (1) An operator shall prevent a companion chatbot on its companion chatbot platform from engaging with users unless the operator maintains a protocol for preventing the production of suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm content to the user, including, but not limited to, by providing a notification to the user that refers the user to crisis service providers, including a suicide hotline or crisis text line, if the user expresses suicidal ideation, suicide, or self-harm.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST have safety protocols before allowing ANY user engagement. Required: (1) protocol to prevent producing suicide/self-harm content, (2) crisis referral notifications when users express suicidal thoughts. This is a mandatory prerequisite to operation.

(2) The operator shall publish details on the protocol required by this subdivision on the operator's internet website.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST publicly disclose their suicide prevention protocols on their website. This provides transparency and accountability.

(c) An operator shall, for a user that the operator knows is a minor, do all of the following:SCOPE: These enhanced protections apply specifically when the operator has knowledge that the user is a minor. Trigger: operator's knowledge of minor status.

  • (1) Disclose to the user that the user is interacting with artificial intelligence.OBLIGATION: For known minors, operators MUST disclose that the chatbot is AI. This is separate from and additional to the general "reasonable person" disclosure requirement.
  • (2) Provide by default a clear and conspicuous notification to the user at least every three hours for continuing companion chatbot interactions that reminds the user to take a break and that the companion chatbot is artificially generated and not human.OBLIGATION: For known minors, operators MUST provide recurring reminders every 3 hours during ongoing interactions. Reminders must include: (1) take a break prompt, (2) AI disclosure. Must be "by default" - can't be opt-in.
  • (3) Institute reasonable measures to prevent its companion chatbot from producing visual material of sexually explicit conduct or directly stating that the minor should engage in sexually explicit conduct.OBLIGATION: For known minors, operators MUST implement "reasonable measures" to block: (1) sexually explicit visual content, (2) statements encouraging the minor to engage in sexual conduct. Standard is "reasonable measures" - not absolute prevention, but meaningful safeguards.
22603. Reporting Requirements

(a) Beginning July 1, 2027, an operator shall annually report to the office all of the following:OBLIGATION: Starting July 1, 2027, operators MUST submit annual reports to the Office of Suicide Prevention. This creates ongoing regulatory oversight and data collection.

  • (1) The number of times the operator has issued a crisis service provider referral notification pursuant to Section 22602 in the preceding calendar year.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST report how many times they triggered crisis referrals. This provides data on suicide prevention intervention frequency.
  • (2) Protocols put in place to detect, remove, and respond to instances of suicidal ideation by users.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST report their protocols for detecting, removing, and responding to suicidal ideation. This ensures ongoing review of safety measures.
  • (3) Protocols put in place to prohibit a companion chatbot response about suicidal ideation or actions with the user.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST report protocols for preventing chatbots from engaging with users on suicide topics. This addresses the chatbot's own content generation.

(b) The report required by this section shall include only the information listed in subdivision (a) and shall not include any identifiers or personal information about users.

(c) The office shall post data from a report required by this section on its internet website.OBLIGATION: The Office of Suicide Prevention MUST publicly post reported data. This creates public transparency about operator safety practices.

(d) An operator shall use evidence-based methods for measuring suicidal ideation.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST use scientifically validated methods to measure suicidal ideation. This ensures detection methods are reliable and effective, not arbitrary.

22604. User Disclosure

An operator shall disclose to a user of its companion chatbot platform, on the application, the browser, or any other format that a user can use to access the companion chatbot platform, that companion chatbots may not be suitable for some minors.OBLIGATION: Operators MUST display a warning that companion chatbots may not be suitable for some minors. Must appear on all access points (app, browser, etc.). This is a general warning separate from minor-specific protections.

22605. Private Right of Action

A person who suffers injury in fact as a result of a violation of this chapter may bring a civil action to recover all of the following relief:ENFORCEMENT: Private right of action - any person injured by violations can sue. Trigger: "injury in fact" (actual concrete harm). This creates private enforcement alongside any government enforcement.

  • (a) Injunctive relief.ENFORCEMENT: Courts can order operators to stop violating the law through injunctions. This prevents ongoing harm.
  • (b) Damages in an amount equal to the greater of actual damages or one thousand dollars ($1,000) per violation.ENFORCEMENT: Plaintiffs can recover either their actual damages OR $1,000 per violation, whichever is greater. The $1,000 statutory minimum provides meaningful penalties even for hard-to-quantify harms.
  • (c) Reasonable attorney's fees and costs.ENFORCEMENT: Winning plaintiffs can recover their legal fees and costs. This makes it economically viable to bring enforcement actions and deters violations.
22606. Cumulative Remedies

The duties, remedies, and obligations imposed by this chapter are cumulative to the duties, remedies, or obligations imposed under other law and shall not be construed to relieve an operator from any duties, remedies, or obligations imposed under any other law.

SEC. 2. Severability

The provisions of this act are severable. If any provision of this act or its application is held invalid, that invalidity shall not affect other provisions or applications that can be given effect without the invalid provision or application.
